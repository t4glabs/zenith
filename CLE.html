CLINICAL LEGAL EDUCATION

CLINICAL LEGAL

EDUCATION

Clinical Legal Education Program aims to build capacities in law schools to work towards grassroot legal empowerment. Law schools were established with the vision of creating social engineers however we seldom see that. We believe there is a need to move beyond the existing rigid clinical legal educational structures and create new pathways to involve law students to instill changemaking capabilities in them to bridge the gap for communities seeking access to justice. Involving law students in clinical work has the potential to help build a robust system of learning by doing, sensitizing and preparing young people to create changemaking capacity and respond to social issues in any spaces (educational or professional) that they occupy.

The touchstone of Zenith’s efforts is catalysed through deliberate and meaningful youth engagement. Through the CLE, the aim is to equip young law students with the practicalities of law, towards changing-making processes, create a platform for students to incubate and experiment with ideas in the field of social justice and build a network of Law School based Legal Services Clinics.

Shivpuri Youth Forum

Legal Aid Clinic

The Shivpuri Youth Forum (SYF) is a youth-led collective aimed at empowering and engaging young people in meaningful activities. Established in 2014, it provides a platform for local youth from Shivpuri to connect, discuss ideas, and tap into their creative potential.

Due to the lack of safe physical spaces in small towns and cities, young people often find themselves isolated. What they need is an environment that sparks inspiration, creativity and innovation. SYF was established with the aim to provide just that environment. The platform not only provides a reading space but also organizes cultural events, workshops, and debates, fostering a space for growth, expression, and breaking stereotypes. SYF leads an environment campaign called "Shivpuri Jalashay Sanrakshan Abhiyaan," with an attempt to restore the rich biodiversity and natural ecosystem of Shivpuri through collective action.

SYF also successfully runs the kalam library - a library and counselling center which provides a reading space to the youth to learn and engage with the aid of materials, newspapers and digital access to resources. The forum's vision is to build local resources and spaces, enabling equal access to opportunities and nurturing young leaders within the community.

Anil Diwan Legal Resource Centre

Anil Divan Foundation has supported the setting up of a legal resource center for law students & young lawyers in Shivpuri. The Foundation has provided infrastructural support in setting up of the center by generously donated books & journals of the Anil B. Divan, who was an eminent jurist and a senior advocate in the Supreme Court.

The center has a remarkable collection of over 1500 books, including authoritative volumes such as the All India Reporter (AIR) manuals , law commission reports and jurisprudence from the United Kingdom (UK) and the United States (US). It also features insightful commentaries on national and international laws.

This generous contribution by the Anil Diwan Foundation ensures that Anil Diwan's extensive knowledge and expertise in the legal field continue to benefit and inspire future generations of legal professionals and also enable Zenith to provide legal aid support to individuals and communities.

Project MPSIC (Madhya Pradesh State Information Commission)

Shri Rahul Singh is an Information Commissioner in the Madhya Pradesh State Information Commission, a statutory body constituted under the Right to Information Act, 2005 (RTI Act, 2005).

Office of Rahul Singh, State Information Commissioner, MPSIC has entered into an MoU with Zenith to design strategies for improving the system of transparency and accountability in Madhya Pradesh. Shri Rahul Singh in his tenure has given many landmark orders to promote transparency regime, started a RTI helpline number for citizens, and even introduced online hearing of cases on WhatsApp and regular calls to make the system accessible to citizens in remote areas.

Through this collaboration Zenith:

  1. a. Provides research assistance in hearing and deciding the appeals and complaints under the Act.
  2. b. Facilitates the Internship Program.
  3. c. Manages the RTI Helpline, a helpline number to help citizens file RTI applications, appeals and create better understanding of the Act.
  4. d. Organise awareness camps, training programs, workshops and seminars, to advance the understanding of the Act and its importance for transparency.
